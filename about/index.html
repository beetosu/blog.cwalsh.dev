Why?

There are a couple of esoteric reasons why I started this blog:

Getting my HTML/writing reps in

I spend a lot of time working on web stuff in my daily life, but I genuinely enjoy learning more about programming outside of the confines of the flashy frameworks everything is needlessly built on. I want this to be a place where I really experiment with all of the weird stuff the HTML/CSS/JS is capable of. The thing I'm trying to find here is a way of writing that is unique to the web.

I want to also spend more time writing in general! I have gotten out of practice and want a outlet where I can just throw out small tidbits.

This is what the internet should be

The more time passes, the crankier I get about the current state of the internet. Social media sucks, search engines suck. In the immortal words of Fred Durst:

In my perfect world, bloggers are a thing again. Information is disseminated through weird, unorganized outlets instead of soulless, algorithmic content farms. The internet should facilitate human interactions, not replace them.
